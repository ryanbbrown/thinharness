# 1984 Summary

George Orwell's *1984* follows Winston Smith, a low-ranking Party worker living under constant surveillance in the totalitarian state of Oceania. As Winston quietly rebels through private thoughts, a forbidden relationship with Julia, and a search for truth, the Party exposes how language, history, fear, and isolation can be used to control society. The novel is a concise warning about authoritarian power and the destruction of individual freedom.
